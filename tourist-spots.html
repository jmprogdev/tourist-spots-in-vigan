Tourist Spots

Calle Crisologo

This is Vigan's most iconic street, lined with ancestral houses from the Spanish colonial era. What to do here is take a walk or, better yet, a kalesa ride, especially at night when the street lamps cast a warm, vintage glow.

History & Trivia

The houses are famous for their bahay na bato architecture, combining stone ground floors and wooden upper floors with sliding windows made of capiz shells. It is said that the street was saved from destruction during World War II because a Japanese officer married to a local woman intervened, preserving the priceless heritage.

Plaza Salcedo

The city's main public square, centrally located and surrounded by important government and religious buildings like the Vigan Cathedral and the Provincial Capitol. The main attraction here is the Dancing Fountain Show held in the evening, show of lights, water, and music.

History & Trivia

Plaza Salcedo is the historical heart of Vigan, named for city founder Juan de Salcedo. It is the central square surrounded by the Vigan Cathedral and the Provincial Capitol. Its most notable historical event was the execution of the revolutionary heroine Gabriela Silang in 1763. A key piece of trivia is its large pool, which was originally installed as a firefighting water reservoir but is now famous for hosting the nightly Dancing Fountain Show.

Plaza Burgos

Located right beside the Vigan Cathedral and Plaza Salcedo, this square is a favorite spot for locals and tourists to relax and enjoy Vigan's famous local street food and lutong bahay. You must try the Vigan Empanada and Okoy here!

History & Trivia

The plaza is named in honor of the native priest and martyr Father Jose Burgos, who was one of the three GomBurZa priests executed by the Spanish.

Cultural and Religious Landmarks

Vigan Cathedral

A majestic Roman Catholic church dominating the central plaza. Its massive structure follows the Earthquake Baroque style, common in the Philippines, which features thick buttresses to withstand earthquakes.

History & Trivia

The current stone church was built in the 18th century. Its octagonal bell tower is separated from the main church building, a strategic design to prevent the tower from crushing the church during a strong earthquake.

Pagburnayan

This is a traditional pottery workshop where you can see the centuries-old method of making the famous burnay or earthen jars. The best thing to do is try your hand at shaping the clay on the potter's wheel.

History & Trivia

The craft of making burnay dates back to the pre-colonial era and was influenced by Chinese traders. The jars are typically used for fermenting local products like basi (sugarcane wine) and bagoong (fish paste).

Ilocos Regional Museum

This museum, housed in the ancestral home of Father Jose Burgos, features historical artifacts, the 14 Basi Revolt paintings (depicting a revolt against the Spanish monopoly on sugarcane wine), and ethnographical materials related to the Ilocos region.

History & Trivia

The house itself is a beautiful example of a wealthy bahay na bato and provides insight into the life of the Filipino elite during the Spanish era. It was also the former location of the Provincial Jail (Carcel), where President Elpidio Quirino was born (his father was a warden).

Unique Destinations

Baluarte Zoo

A government-owned and managed mini-zoo and conservation center, famous for its various animals, including ostriches, tigers, and a number of exotic birds. A must-do is watching the Animal Show.

History & Trivia

Owned by former Ilocos Sur Governor Luis "Chavit" Singson, it is often referred to as a "safari gallery" and advocates for animal welfare and conservation.

Hidden Garden

Despite its name, this place is well-known and is a relaxing spot that functions as a nursery for various plants and a restaurant serving classic Ilocano cuisine. The thing to do here is to relax and have a snack or meal.

History & Trivia

It was developed by a local couple, Francis and Maria Goulart, and is known for its quiet, provincial ambiance, offering a tranquil escape from the bustling city center.

One Ilocos Sur Cafe

While not a historic landmark, a cafe like this, which is often found within a restored ancestral house, is a great place to experience the modern-day local culture while appreciating the classic architecture. The best thing to do is try their local coffee blends and pastries.

History & Trivia

The adaptive reuse of old heritage structures into commercial spaces like cafes and restaurants helps fund the preservation of Vigan's unique architectural heritage, allowing tourists to appreciate the interiors of these old homes.
